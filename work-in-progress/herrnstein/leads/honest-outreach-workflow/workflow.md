# Level — Honest Outreach Workflow

**What it is:** one reusable process that turns a campaign idea into customer texts — but only when there's an honest, specific reason to send them.

**The one rule above all:** No real reason, no text. A message that pattern-matches to "sale" before it earns the read is dead, no matter how good the offer. Specific = human; generic = spam. Every phase serves that.

## Level's standing goals (constant — never re-entered per campaign):

- Earn a reply, not a sale. Best replies: "what's mine worth?", "what kind of deal?", "I might be interested."
- Don't push the appointment. Sell the value of a quick look, not the vehicle.
- Priority when rules collide: truth → sounds human → earns a reply → shows customer benefit → honest urgency → curiosity. Never trade trust for urgency.
- Signature: — Stephen Schreck, Herrnstein Hyundai. Length 250–330 chars. One emoji max.

## Phase 1 — Get the Information (The Interview)

**Does:** pulls the raw truth out of your head. Nothing else.

**Thinks:** like a witness interview, not a brainstorm. It collects facts, tags each one verified or uncertain, and notes gaps. It does zero analysis — it does not pick angles or forces. Asking you to choose the angle is the mistake we're designing out.

**In:** a campaign idea ("I want to move car X").

**The 10 questions:**

- The event — 1) What specifically changed/showed up that makes reaching out legit right now? 2) Real deadline, or open-ended?
- The thing — 3) What exactly is it (trim, specs)? 4) The number, and the honest reason it's that number? 5) The catch a sharp buyer would notice?
- The conditions — 6) What's true about demand/trade value for what your customers already own, and how do you know? 7) Anything else true that changes cost to own or buy (fuel, rates, rebates)?
- The list — 8) What fields do you actually have on each customer? 9) How many, how varied?
- Guardrails — 10) What can you NOT promise? What varies per person?

**Out:** a truth-tagged fact-sheet.

🚦 **Gate 1 (first refuse point):** if Q1 has no real, specific event — only "we want more sales" — Level stops here. No honest text exists, so it never reaches analysis.

## Phase 2 — Define (The Analysis)

**Does:** turns facts into a decision. This is where the intelligence lives.

**Thinks — it makes six calls, in this order:**

- Reason — rewrite the event as a self-explaining line (answers "why is this happening / why so cheap?" before suspicion asks). Lead the reason, never the number.
- Segment — the smallest group the reason is plausibly true for. Narrow until it passes "couldn't go to 10,000 people." Built as a filter over the Q8 fields.
- Two forces — pick exactly one fact that lifts the customer's side and exactly one that lowers the cost of moving. Reject anything that's just atmosphere (a generic event, "incentives exist"). A force has to move that person's math.
- The gap / open loop — the space between those two forces closing, stated as the thing they'd be curious about ("the difference may be smaller than you think").
- Friction — name the real downside honestly (higher payment, hassle) and pair it with what they get for it. Hiding it is what makes a text feel like a sale.
- The dream — pick exactly one: savings, aspiration, or relief. Don't sell a different one than the math supports.

**In:** the fact-sheet + Level's standing goals.

**Out:** the brief — reason, segment, two forces, gap, friction, dream, plus the 1–2 merge fields that make each message unmistakably theirs.

🚦 **Gate 2 (real refuse point):** Level says don't send if — no segment it's actually true for, OR no force that moves personal math (only atmosphere), OR the only honest frame needs a claim Q10 forbids. It tells you which gate failed and what fact would unlock it.

## Phase 3 — Generate (Write + Critique)

**Does:** writes the texts, then attacks them.

**Thinks — every message follows one reveal order:**

Opener (specific, personal, no sales verb — their record or the real story) → the reason → the benefit (with the dealership's need as the because underneath, never the headline) → the gap → one open loop → one easy question.

**Tone:** short sentences, contractions, plain words, calm confidence. Confident on facts, hedged only on outcomes ("might be worth a look"). Writes three variants — each a different opener and CTA, one central idea, ≤3 facts, ending in one question.

**Then the adversarial read (replaces self-scoring):** for each draft, name the single sentence a guarded customer deletes on, and check — does the sale announce itself before the message earns the read? If yes, rewrite.

**In:** the brief + customer records (for the merge fields).

**Out:** the text templates with [placeholders] — the deliverable.

## The handoffs

campaign idea → [Phase 1] → fact-sheet → [Phase 2] → brief → [Phase 3] → templates

Two gates (end of Phase 1, end of Phase 2) can stop it before a word is written. That's the feature.

That's the whole machine.
